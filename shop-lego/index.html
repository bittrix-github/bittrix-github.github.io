about the app

As a big part of my web development portfolio version 1.0 i decided to go for a fictional e-store that i could explore some basic functions in a shopping cart. I just needed to come up with ideas of products that could sell by type, quantity and color so i could follow up my prototype intention.

Struggled to even start the project just because i couldn't think of any decent theme then one day it just came out so obvious: a retail shop for Lego parts. Actually it kind of exists already and i was really blown away when i discovered a couple of years ago those european Lego stores where you can buy parts by weight. At the end of the day it fitted perfectly for both me and the project, as i grew up as a kid building mecha robots and Formula One cars with Lego.

Aok, the application is pretty straight forward: you the pick the part, you choose color and quantity then you add to the cart. I feel like i really evolved making this over one week span because i had to go through lots of different examples to try and make it work properly and then, finally, it works pretty much exactly as i was expecting it to.

You can add items to the cart (this little counter will always let you aware of how many items are in there), you can delete items from the cart, update the quantity in the cart and ultimately close the deal - which thanks the customer and clears the cart. I worked on a fair amount of functions to not just add or delete items, but to create divs innerHTML, update the prices by changing quantities, update the total price of the purchase and update the counter shown on the cart icon. One defensive method is to prevent repeated items, so when a part and color are already in the cart it pops up an alert to the user so he/she can review the action.

Well, i guess this is it. Enjoy fake buying cool Lego parts - as it's really depressing realizing that is not a real thing :(

P.S.: i had to design and vectorize all the 22 different parts from scratch, and it was really painful and fun at the same time. =p
